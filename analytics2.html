Rivervale Housing Price Analysis

INTRODUCTION

Rivervale has experienced unprecedented growth in recent years, attracting a surge of residents seeking a harmonious blend of urban convenience and natural beauty. Real estate professionals, policymakers, and enthusiasts are eager to understand the factors influencing property values in this unique city.

OBJECTIVE

To unravel patterns, correlations, and insights that will shape the city's housing landscape by exploring data.

DATASET

Attribute Description
price The price of the property in Rivervale.
area The area or size of the property.
lakefront A binary variable indicating whether the property is located on the lakefront.
bedrooms The number of bedrooms in the property.
bathrooms The number of bathrooms in the property.
stories The number of stories or levels in the property.
mainroad A binary variable indicating whether the property is accessible from the main road.
guestroom A binary variable indicating whether the property has a guest room.
basement A binary variable indicating whether the property has a basement.
hotwaterheating A binary variable indicating whether the property has hot water heating.
airconditioning A binary variable indicating whether the property has air conditioning.
parking The availability of parking spaces for the property.
prefarea A binary variable indicating whether the property is in a preferred area.
furnishingstatus The furnishing status of the property.

Next, checked correlations to understand the relationship between 'price', the target variable, and the other numerical variables. Based on the result of the normality test, applied Spearman's method. 

 > chart.Correlation(df_num, method = "spearman")

All of the numerical variables have a positive correlation with price, with significant p-values. 'area' and 'bathrooms' especially have a strong one. It indicates that the larger area the house has / the more bathrooms the house has, the higher the price becomes, and the impacts are bigger than the other numerical variables.
