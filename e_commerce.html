UK E-commerce Analysis

INTRODUCTION

This is a sales transaction data set of UK-based e-commerce (online retail) for one year. This London-based shop has been selling gifts and homewares for adults and children through the website since 2007. Their customers come from all over the world and usually make direct purchases for themselves. There are also small businesses that buy in bulk and sell to other customers through retail outlet channels.

OBJECTIVE

To understand the business situation, specifically address the following questions:

How was the sales trend over the year?

How many customers purchased in each month?

Which products were popular?

How was the sales amount ratio by country?

Which country had high average sales per customer?

DATASET

Attribute Description
TransactionNo a six-digit unique number that defines each transaction. The letter “C” in the code indicates a cancellation.
Date the date when each transaction was generated.
ProductNo a five or six-digit unique character used to identify a specific product.
Product product/item name.
Price the price of each product per unit in pound sterling (£).
Quantity the quantity of each product per transaction. Negative values related to cancelled transactions.
CustomerNo a five-digit unique number that defines each customer.
Country name of the country where the customer resides.

DATA PREPARATION

  • I found that the data contains 55 Null Value in CostomerNo and that they are cancellation transactions. To calculate the accurate sales amount, I didn't drop these rows.
  • Changed data type of “TransactionNo” and “CustomerNo” from numeric to string to avoid errors.
  • Created 'Sales' column(Price*Quantity).

Which country had high average sales per customer?

/*Price per Customer Bar Chart*/

According to the comparison by country, the UK had the highest rate with 82.94% of sales of all the countries, since this e-commerce is based in the UK. However, the average sales per customer is higher in Netherlands, EIRE, Australia, Singapore, Sweden, Iceland, Japan, Norway, Switzerland, and France. This might be because these countries have small number of customers and they are retail businesses, but this chart suggests that the products the e-commerce sells can be popular in other countries. Finding retailers in other countries and extending the market can be an effective strategy.

SUMMARY

  • September saw a significant sales spike, suggesting the need for further investigation into the contributing factors, such as marketing initiatives.
  • The top-selling product, "popcorn holder," underscores the importance of understanding consumer preferences for developing successful products.
  • While the UK leads in total sales, countries like Netherlands, EIRE, Australia, Singapore, Sweden, Iceland, Japan, Norway, Switzerland, and France show higher average sales per customer. This presents opportunities for market expansion and targeted strategies to reach new customers.
